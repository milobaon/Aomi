Artist Statement Milo Baón

My work does not answer that question. It pushes it to its limit until it breaks on its own.

Every animal starts as a body. It ends as a product. I stay in the middle — in that exact instant when it is still both things at once.

I work from El Rompido, Spain. Here the sea is not landscape — it is economy, identity, and symptom. Crabs inhabit estuaries where hotels discharge their wastewater. Anchovies ingest microplastics that end up on our tables. The cycle is perfect in its absurdity. Nobody planned it. Nobody stops it.

There are no villains in these paintings. What there is instead is a mirror. A double unconsciousness: the octopus does not know it is inside the jar. We prefer not to know that we are the ones who put it there.

"I paint with all the love and all the skill I have.
And then I place the world on top.
To see if anyone recognizes it."

— Milo Baón
About the Artist

She learned to look
when she could
not listen.

At five years old, Milo Baón lost eighty percent of her hearing. The world kept talking. She found another way to answer it — she painted. Not as therapy, but because it was the only language in which no one asked her to repeat herself.

Seven surgeries later, painting never stopped being her mother tongue. Then her father died. For two years she did not paint — not because she didn't want to, but because the language she had was no longer capable of saying what was new.

Then she moved to El Rompido. The sea did what nature had always done: it was there, asking nothing. She looked differently. AOMI was born.
